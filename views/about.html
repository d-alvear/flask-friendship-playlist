I'm a Data Science Fellow at SharpestMinds

SharpestMinds is a mentorship platform that pairs aspiring data scientists with professionals in the field. I built Friendshipify under the guidance of my mentor Rocio Ng. Click here to read more about how the project was built.

My Background

I received a Master's Degree in Geosciences from San Francisco State University in 2019. My thesis quantified the relationship between climate and hydrologic processes to understand the impacts of climate change on water resources used for agriculture.

During my Master's program, I discovered that I really enjoyed performing statistical analyses and finding ways to quantitatively answer open ended questions about Earth's physical processes. After looking into my options after graduation, I came to the realization that I wasn't excited or particularly interested in the work I would be expected to do as a geologist and had no idea what I wanted to do instead. A friend suggested I look into data science and learn the basics of Python to see if data science is something I would be interested in.

I started with a Coursera specialization and was hooked. After the specialization, I spent a few months working through Dataquest's Data Science Track to sharpen my Python skills and get more experience with the popular tools and libraries. After 5 months of learning, 30 ignored job applications, and reading way too many articles on transitioning into data science, I realized I needed some guidance. One piece of advice I took away from reading all those articles was to get a mentor. I came across SharpestMinds on Towards Data Science and it seemed to be exactly the kind of guidance I was looking for. I started my mentorship with Rocio Ng in November 2019, and have been working on building Friendshipify since then.
