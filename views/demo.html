Demo Cases

Demo 1: Pop vs. Rock

Two friends, Donna and Jackie, both like different music. Jackie loves 2000s pop and Mariah Carey's early music. Donna prefers rock from the 90's to early 2000s. They decide to see what songs Friendshipify would recommend to them.

Jackie's Pop Songs:

  • Bad Romance, Lady Gaga
  • Fantasy, Mariah Carey
  • Cry Me a River, Justin Timberlake

Donna's Rock Songs:

  • Smells Like Teen Spirit, Nirvana
  • Island In the Sun, Weezer
  • Never Let You Go, Third Eye Blind

Results:

  • The Chamber, Lenny Kravitz, pop
  • Beverly Hills, Weezer, rock
  • Semi-Charmed Life, Third Eye Blind, rock
  • My Wave, Soundgarden, rock
  • MMMBop, Hanson, pop
  • Still Got Me, Daniel Merriweather, pop
  • Good In Goodbye, Madison Beer, pop

Plot Features:

From the Spotify API Docs

  • Danceability: how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength and overall regularity. A value of 0.0 is least danceable, and 1.0 is most danceable.
  • Energy: a measure from 0.0 to 1.0 and represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy. For example, death metal has high energy, while a Bach prelude scores low on the scale.
  • Tempo: the overall estimated tempo of a track in beats per minute (BPM). In musical terminology, tempo is the speed or pace of a given piece and derives directly from the average beat duration.
  • Valence: a measure from 0.0 to 1.0 describing the musical positiveness conveyed by a track. Tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g. happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry).

Plot Explanation:

This grouped violin plot compares the audio features of Jackie and Donna's songs. The box plot inside the violin shows how the features of both friends' songs are distributed, while the curve of the violin represents the probability of all songs in the genre taking on the associated feature value. In other words, if Jackie and Donna each picked a fourth song, there is a higher probability their fourth song will take on a value where the violin is widest, and a lower probability of their song having a value where the violin is the skinniest. These probabilities can be seen by hovering over the violin and seeing how the kernel density estimate (kde) changes as you move along the violin.

Jackie's pop songs have a lower tempo, are moderately danceable, have varied energy, and are moderately positive. All Donna's rock songs appear to have the same low tempo, are less danceable than Jackie's pop songs, have high energy and are moderately positive.

The distribution of values for the songs recommended by Friendshipify appear to visually match the distributions of Jackie and Donna's songs. The recommended songs have the same low tempo as Jackie and Donna's songs, as well as the varied positiveness. The energy values of the recommendations seem to encompass the range of values seen in both friends' songs. However, there is a higher probability of Friendshipify recommending high energy songs like Donna's. The danceability values of the recommendations is interesting due to the inclusion of two extreme values of 0.88 and 0.51, despite the rest of the songs falling within the mean danceability values for both Jackie and Donna. Friendshipify could be including these extreme values to address the 50% probability of Jackie's next song having a danceability value of 0.88 or the fact that one of Donna's songs has a danceability value of 0.51.

Despite the differences in danceability, the results seem to be good ones based on the plotted features. I feel that the songs audibly match. Lenny Kravitz's "The Chamber" sounds very pop/rock, so I feel that its a song that would appeal to both Jackie and Donna. "MMMBop" matches the era of both Jackie and Donna's picks and sounds similar to Third Eye Blind's "Never Let You Go" and "Semi-Charmed Life", the latter being recommended from Donna's songs. "Good In Goodbye" is being recommended from Jackie's picks and sounds very similar to Justin Timberlake's "Cry Me A River."



Demo 2: Rock vs. Hip Hop

Donna and Jackie's friend Hyde also likes the same kind of rock music as Donna. They ask their friend Fez what kind of music he likes. He likes hip hop and his two favorite groups are OutKast and TLC. Fez also thought Drake's song "In My Feelings" was pretty catchy when it came out. Rock and hip hop are very distinct genres, so Hyde and Fez see what kinds of songs Friendshipify recommends to them.

Hyde's Rock Songs:

  • The Middle, Jimmy Eat World
  • Mr. Brightside, The Killers
  • Californication, Red Hot Chili Peppers

Fez's Hip Hop Songs:

  • Hey Ya, OutKast
  • In My Feelings, Drake
  • Waterfalls, TLC

Results:

  • One Dance, Drake, hip hop
  • Hold Me Down, Motion City Soundtrack, rock
  • The Royal We, Silversun Pickups, rock
  • Tones Of Home, Blind Melon, rock
  • Three Is A Magic Number, Blind Melon, rock
  • Backyard, Kota the Friend, hip hop
  • Sucker M.C.'s, Run-D.M.C., hip hop

Demo 3: Mixed Genres

The four friends decide to ask their friends Eric and Kelso about the kind of music they like. Eric likes indie songs while Kelso loves funk, Marvin Gaye is his favorite artist. The group wonders what songs Friendshipify would give if they put in songs from all their favorite genres. To make things interesting, Fez decides to put in an ABBA song and Donna changes her genre to classic rock.

Jackie, Fez, and Hyde's Songs:

  • Call Me Maybe, Carly Rae Jepsen
  • Dancing Queen, ABBA
  • All The Small Things, blink-182

Eric, Kelso, and Donna's Songs:

  • Electric Feel, MGMT
  • Got To Give It Up, Marvin Gaye
  • Hotel California, Eagles

Results:

  • Post Malone, Sam Feldt, pop
  • Bad To You, Ariana Grande, pop
  • Blow Me, The Used, rock
  • Pretty Peggy, Wolfmother, rock
  • Fortunate Son, Creedence Clearwater Revival, classic rock
  • Walls, ROZES, indie

This grouped violin plot compares the audio features of each group's songs. Since the songs provided all come from different genres, the resulting songs are also variable.
